One Image x 5

By: Samantha Mayer

For this version of the image, you must look to the background of the entire page. I not only assigned the image to the background, but also added unusual red shaped with slight opacity. I did this to have the image represent the manifesto and it's ideas as consistent throughout, but to have the unusual image make it confusing/convoluted to look at which is how I felt while reading it. While the ideas may have been consistant and easily recognizable, I felt as though a lot of the depth to the writing was lost on me.

Say hi, Frank!

This image version has been enlarged to represent the magnitude of the concept being played with; further complex due to the time in which the manifesto was written. I also feel that the enlargement of this photo embodies how "in your face" the ideas of the text felt as a reader.

Say hi, Frank!

For this version, I decided to use the tile effect. The repetition of this image felt indicative of the repatition of ideas restated throughout the manifesto as if to be knew than the previous pages, but seemed (at their core), the same.

Say hi, Frank!

For this version, I have adjusted the brightness, contrast, and saturation level by manipulating the filter. I have also used the mix-blend-mode to multiply 50%. These manipulations of the image are significant because the increase of contrast can be understood as a critic to the one-sidedness of perspectives at play in the manifesto. While characteristic of a manifesto, it made the reading seem overdone; just as the colours of the photo have been overdone and the once appealing picture is not easily recognizable.
